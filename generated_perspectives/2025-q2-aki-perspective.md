## Re-think ...

**Here's something Aki may want to re-think:**

When expertise runs deep, solutions can come to mind before problems are fully understood. I noticed that during sprint planning sessions, particularly when the PM was asking for estimates under deadline pressure, Aki sometimes jumped to technical implementation details before fully exploring the customer problem. This solution-first tendency, while coming from a place of wanting to be helpful, occasionally led to initial skepticism toward alternative approaches that differed from Aki's first instinct.

**Here's an example to consider for doing it another way:**

Try **exercising curiosity** by asking "What problem are we really solving for the customer?" before proposing solutions, even when time feels short. Consider being the person who says "Tell me more about why that approach appeals to you" when teammates suggest alternatives—this creates space for collaborative exploration and models the psychological safety Aki already creates so well in other contexts.
