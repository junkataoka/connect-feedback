## Keep doing ... (mandatory)

**Here's something I think Muruganandam does really well and hope Muruganandam keeps doing:**

## Re-think ... (optional)

**Here's something Muruganandam may want to re-think:**

A lot of the coordination heartbeat ran directly through Muruganandam this quarter — chasing slide updates, mapping subtasks, confirming availability, prepping every CPR. In an engagement with daily standups, multiple ceremonies, and cross-company stakeholders, **practicing awareness** of how naturally TPM roles concentrate this work matters; it's not a personal pattern as much as a structural one. The risk is that the team learns to wait for Muruganandam's nudge rather than self-driving.

**Here's an example to consider for doing it another way:**

What if the next sprint started with a one-pager that assigned an **accountable workstream lead** for each track — owning the status entry, the CPR slide, and the customer follow-up directly — with Muruganandam moving into a reviewer/escalation role? Even one sprint of running it that way would test whether the **courage** to let some balls almost-drop unlocks more durable team ownership.
